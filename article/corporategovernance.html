Corporate Governance

Corporate Governance refers to the set of systems, principles and processes by which a company is governed. They provide guidelines as to how the company can be directed or controlled such that it can fulfill its goals and objectives in a manner that adds to the value of the company and is also beneficial for all stakeholferss in the long term. Stakeholders in this case would include everyone ranging from Board of Directors, management, shareholders, customers, employees and society. The management of the company hence assumes role of a trustee for all the others.

Corporate governance is based on the principles such as conducting the business with all integrity and fairness, being transparent with regard to all transactions, making all necessary disclosures and decisions, complying with all laws of land, accountability and responsibility towards stakeholders and commitment to conduting business in an ethical manner.

Objective and Importance of Corporate Governance

Fundamentally, there is a level of confidence that is associated with a company that is known to have good governance. The presence of an active group of independent directors on board contributes a great deak towards ensuring confidence in the market. Corporate governance is known to be one of the criteria that foreign institutional investors are increasingly depending on when deciding on which companies to invest in. It is also known to have a positive influence on share price of the company. Having a clean image on the corporate governance could make it easier for companies to source capital at more reasonable costs. It has the following objectives and importance.

  1. Adequate disclosures and transparency for effective decision making
  2. Statutory legal complainces
  3. Protections of shareholders interests
  4. Commitment to values and ethical conduct of business
  5. Management of risk
  6. Make risks more predictable
  7. Clarify the authority -- responsibility -- accountabilty
  8. Avoidance of personal or group interests taking over preduent decisions
  9. Adequate internal control over day to day business
  10. Enhance stakeholder confidence
  11. Encourage for long run benefit which must be fair
  12. Ensures qualified Board of Directors
  13. Effective monitoring and control

Principles of Corporate Governance

Corporate Governance: Need and Significance in Nepalese Banking System

Corporate governance is a combination of corporate policies and best practices adopted by corporate bodies to achieve its objectives in relation to their stakehokders. It is also the field economies which studies the many issues arising from the separation from ownership and control. The fundamental objective of corporate governance reforms is to enhance transparency and transparency enhances accountability. It is widely recognized that transparency enhance trust among the major players within governance framework. Various definition principles have been introduced to stabilize corporate governnance among corporate entities.

  • Corporate governance is the system by which companies are directed and controlled
  • Set of relationship between a company's management, its boards, its shareholders ad other stakeholders

In brief, corporate governance is a set of process a entity's culture, policies, laws and institutional value that affect way a corporation is directed, administrated or controlled. It is a combination of corporate policies and best practices adpoted by corporated bodies in achieving its objectives in relation to their stakeholders. It aims to protect shareholder's rights to enhance disclosure and transparency, to facilitate effective functioning of the board and to provide an efficient legal and regulatory enforcement framework. It addresses the principale/agency problem through a mix of company law, stock exchange rules and sub regulatory codes.

  3. Banks have wider stakeholders, government, regulators and most importantly depositors
  4. Promoted market confidence, helps to attract additional capital, fosters market discipline through good disclosures and transparency.
